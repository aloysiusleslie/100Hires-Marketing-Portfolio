### Summary
Chris Orlob explains that many professionals stay busy with low-value activities and mistake effort for progress. He argues that working harder is not enough if the work itself does not create meaningful business outcomes. Instead of maximizing activity, people should focus on developing high-value skills and prioritizing tasks that directly impact growth, revenue, or customer success.

### Key Insight
Success comes from working on the right things, not simply working harder. High-leverage skills and activities create significantly better results than increasing effort on low-value tasks.

### Summary
Chris Orlob argues that the fastest way to close enterprise deals is often to slow down the early stages of the sales process. Instead of rushing to a demo or proposal, sales professionals should invest time in understanding the customer's business, uncovering root problems, building a strong point of view, and engaging multiple stakeholders.
