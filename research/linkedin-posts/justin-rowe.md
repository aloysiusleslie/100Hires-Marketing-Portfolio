Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justin-rowe-4043339b_proving-linkedin-influenced-a-deal-isnt-that-activity-7472669328820027393-WPb1

### Summary
Justin Rowe explains that proving LinkedIn's influence on closed deals is often more straightforward than many marketers assume. He argues that buyers frequently engage with LinkedIn content throughout their purchasing journey, even when the platform is not directly credited in attribution reports. By analyzing customer conversations, engagement patterns, and self-reported attribution data, companies can better understand LinkedIn's role in driving awareness, trust, and pipeline generation.

### Key Insight
Not all marketing impact appears in attribution software. LinkedIn can significantly influence buying decisions long before a prospect converts into a measurable lead or customer.

## Post 2

Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justin-rowe-4043339b_before-you-spend-another-dollar-on-cold-linkedin-activity-7468365302762737664-SE3i

### Summary
Justin Rowe advises companies to evaluate their LinkedIn presence and content strategy before investing more money into LinkedIn advertising. He explains that prospects often review a company's profile, leadership team, and content before engaging with ads or sales outreach. A weak organic presence can reduce the effectiveness of paid campaigns, while a strong brand presence increases trust and conversion rates.

### Key Insight
Paid advertising performs best when supported by a strong organic presence. Building credibility and trust through content can significantly improve the return on ad spend.
