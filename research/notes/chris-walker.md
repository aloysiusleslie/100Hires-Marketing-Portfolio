## Video 1

**Title:** The Future of GTM and Sustainable Growth with Chris Walker, CEO at Passetto

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lBfhsw3OaGk

**Channel:** Exit Five

**Publication date:** September 10, 2025

**Main topic:** The shift from growth-at-all-costs to sustainable GTM, how marketing should be measured and structured, and the role of AI in B2B marketing teams.

**5 key takeaways:**

1. The macro shift in B2B is from growth-at-all-costs to sustainable, efficient growth—requiring marketing, SDRs, and sales to operate as one integrated machine rather than siloed functions chasing attribution credit.
2. Podcasts and social media deliver strategic value beyond pipeline: real-time customer feedback, objection handling, message testing, and retention/expansion alignment—not just lead generation.
3. Marketing ROI must include all costs (headcount, agencies, tech, SDRs, sales)—not just ad platform ROAS. Celebrating a 3x Google Ads return while ignoring total GTM cost can mask a five-year CAC payback.
4. Marketing should split into two functions: **strategy** (positioning, research, competitive intel, thought leadership) and **pipeline creation** (demand creation, supply chain, prospecting)—each measured on different timelines and KPIs.
5. Smaller, AI-enabled marketing teams (often under 10 people) can outperform 50–100 person teams by reducing meeting bloat; AI is an accelerant for already-solid strategy, not a substitute for it.

## Video 2

**Title:** The shift in B2B buying you can't afford to ignore ⚠️ #b2b #marketingtips

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6Orr5znB9FY

**Channel:** Refine Labs

**Publication date:** June 8, 2026

**Main topic:** The Brand, Demand, Expand three-pillar GTM framework for building predictable B2B SaaS revenue.

**5 key takeaways:**

1. B2B buying has shifted across three eras (sales-dependent → internet-enabled → AI-accelerated); winners understand customers and show up at every stage of the journey, not only when someone raises a hand.
2. Brand's job is to be in the consideration set before buying starts—research from Gartner, BCG, and 6sense cited in the video shows 85% of buyers already have 2–3 brands in mind, and 90% of purchases come from that initial list.
3. Demand has two types: **capture** (buyers actively in-market) and **creation** (the ~95% not ready yet). Most companies overfund capture and underfund creation, causing lumpy pipeline.
4. **Expand** (retention, upsell, cross-sell, advocacy) is the most overlooked pillar—most companies hand post-sale entirely to customer success and miss expansion revenue.
5. A healthier budget allocation is roughly 50–60% demand, 20–30% brand, and 10–20% expand—versus the typical 80% demand / tiny brand / zero expand split.

## Video 3

**Title:** The 20/40/40 Split That Fixed Our Pipeline #DemandGen #B2BSaaS

**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RGMtRn0wrXo

**Channel:** Refine Labs

**Publication date:** April 30, 2026

**Main topic:** Paid media budget allocation—why most B2B companies over-invest in Google Search and how a 20/40/40 split (Google/LinkedIn/Meta) creates a compounding demand system.

**5 key takeaways:**

1. Google Search only captures 1–3% of your total addressable market at any given time (buyers already in-market). You cannot grow that pool by spending more on search alone.
2. Paid social (LinkedIn, Meta) reaches the other 97–99%—building awareness and preferences long before buyers type a search query.
3. Refine Labs recommends a rough budget split of ~20% Google Search, ~40% LinkedIn, and ~40% Meta—the principle is that search should make up less than social, not more.
4. Restructuring Google to eliminate low-intent waste frees budget that can be redirected to fund paid social demand creation.
5. The compounding effect: buyers who've seen your brand on LinkedIn arrive warmer when they search—branded impression share, non-brand quality scores, and cost-per-hero improve as a result.
